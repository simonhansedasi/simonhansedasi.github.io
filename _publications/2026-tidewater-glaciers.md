Using curated datasets and a shallow neural network trained on glacier-averaged thickness measurements, we quantify the imprint of glacier–ocean and glacier–lake interactions on global non-ice sheet glacier volume. We find that tidewater and lake-terminating glaciers are systematically thicker than land-terminating glaciers — an effect that accounts for approximately 20% of non-ice-sheet global glacier volume. Results are interpreted through a mechanical model whereby water pressure at the ice front permits thicker termini than are stable in air, consistent with theoretical limits on ice cliff height.
